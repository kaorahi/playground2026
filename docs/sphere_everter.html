Sphere Everter

Turn a sphere inside out without cutting it, puncturing it, or making creases. The surface is allowed to pass through itself along the way.

Allowed and forbidden operations

OperationAllowed?Reason
Stretch or shrinkYesIt is a smooth deformation.
Let the surface pass through itselfYesSelf-intersections are allowed.
Cut or punctureNoThis changes the topology of the sphere.
Make a sharp creaseNoThis breaks smoothness.
Collapse the surface to a pointNoThe local surface structure is destroyed.

References

The formulas in this demo are based on PyVista’s “Turning the sphere inside out” example and Adam Bednorz and Witold Bednorz, “Analytic sphere eversion using ruled surfaces”. Globe mode uses an embedded 360×180 land/sea mask derived from Natural Earth 1:110m land polygons.

  • PyVista: Turning the sphere inside out
  • Adam Bednorz and Witold Bednorz: Analytic sphere eversion using ruled surfaces
  • Natural Earth: 1:110m Physical Vectors — Land (Globe mode land/sea source; Public Domain. Terms: Natural Earth Terms of Use)
